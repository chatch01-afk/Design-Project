1.Drake

Aubrey Drake Graham was born on October 24, 1986, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, to an African-American musician father, Dennis Graham, and a Canadian Jewish mother, Sandra "Sandi" Graham. He grew up primarily with his mother in Toronto’s Forest Hill neighborhood and attended a Jewish day school, becoming bar mitzvah at age 13, while maintaining a connection to his father’s musical background.

2.Bad Bunny

Born Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio on March 10, 1994, in Vega Baja, Puerto Rico, Bad Bunny grew up in a musically rich environment, with his mother often playing salsa, merengue, and ballads. He began singing in a church choir at a young age and started writing his own songs around age 13, inspired by artists like Daddy Yankee.

3.Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift (born December 13, 1989, in West Reading, Pennsylvania) is a multitalented singer-songwriter and global superstar known for her heartfelt lyrics and catchy melodies. She has captivated audiences and solidified herself as one of the most influential artists in contemporary music, recognized for her autobiographical songwriting and artistic reinventions. Swift is also the highest-grossing live music artist and one of the best-selling music artists of all time.

4.The Weeknd

The Weeknd first gained attention in 2010 when he anonymously uploaded several songs to YouTube. His 2011 mixtapes—House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence—were critically acclaimed and established him as a prominent figure in the music scene. These works showcased his unique sound characterized by atmospheric production and confessional lyrics, often exploring themes of love, drug use, and alienation

5. Billie Eilish

Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell, born December 18, 2001 is an American singer-songwriter. Known for her distinctive musical sound and vocal style, Eilish is a prominent figure in 2020s pop culture. She first gained public attention in 2015 with her debut single "Ocean Eyes" which was released on SoundCloud and written and produced by her brother Finneas O'Connell
